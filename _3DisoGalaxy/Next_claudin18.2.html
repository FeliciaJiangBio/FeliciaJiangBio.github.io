üìåI. Target Origin and Basic Biological Mechanism

1.1 Discovery History of the Claudin18 Gene

The Claudin18 gene was first discovered in 1998 as an important member of the tight junction protein family. Through in-depth research into the structure and function of tight junction proteins, scientists identified two key splice variants of Claudin18: Claudin18.1 and Claudin18.2.

The significance of this discovery lies in revealing the molecular basis of tissue-specific expression:

  • Claudin18.1: Primarily expressed in lung tissue, involved in tight junctions between alveolar epithelial cells.
  • Claudin18.2: Specifically expressed in differentiated epithelial cells of the gastric mucosa. This unique tissue distribution laid the foundation for subsequent targeted therapies.

1.2 Mechanism of Claudin18.2's Gastric Epithelial Specific Expression

Claudin18.2, as a four-transmembrane protein, holds profound biological significance due to its specific expression in the gastric epithelium:

Molecular Structural Features:

  • Belongs to the tight junction protein family, containing four transmembrane domains.
  • Its primary function is to maintain cell barrier integrity and cell polarity.
  • In normal gastric mucosa, it is exclusively expressed within the tight junctions of differentiated epithelial cells.

Uniqueness of Expression Pattern:

  • Not expressed in gastric stem cell regions; found only in differentiated gastric epithelial cells.
  • This differentiation-dependent expression pattern makes it an ideal tumor biomarker.
  • Under normal conditions, it is tightly sealed within the intercellular tight junction complex.

1.3 Epitope Exposure Mechanism in Cancer Cells

The transformation of Claudin18.2 from "hidden" to "exposed" is pivotal to its success as a therapeutic target:

"Hidden" in Normal State:

  • Located at the very top of the intercellular space.
  • Binds with Claudin18.2 molecules on adjacent cell surfaces to form tight junction complexes.
  • Buried within the supramolecular complex of tight junctions, making it inaccessible to antibodies.

"Exposed" in Tumor State:

  • Malignant tumor development leads to the disruption of tight junction structures.
  • The Claudin18.2 epitope on the tumor cell surface becomes fully exposed.
  • It thereby becomes a target that antibody drugs can specifically recognize and bind to.

1.4 Milestone Discovery of Tumor-Selective Expression

Groundbreaking Research:

  • Research by Sentani Kazuhiro et al. in 2008 first described the stable high expression of Claudin18.2 in gastric cancer.
  • In the same year, crucial research by U. ≈ûahin et al. highlighted Claudin18.2's highly selective expression across various cancer types.

Discovery of Broad Expression Profile:

  • Gastric Cancer: The predominant cancer type expressing this target.
  • Pancreatic Cancer: Exhibits significant abnormal activation.
  • Esophageal Cancer, Ovarian Cancer, Lung Cancer: Shows aberrant activation in various solid tumors.
  • This broad expression characteristic substantially expands its therapeutic application prospects.
